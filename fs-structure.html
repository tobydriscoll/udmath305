Section 3.1 Structure of linear ODE systems

¶

Most phenomena in the real world do not have just one dependent variable.

Example 3.1.1.

Consider two connected tanks holding brine. Tank 1 holds 100 L, has an input of 4 L/hr of brine of concentration 3 kg/L, and an output of 6 L/hr. Tank 2 holds 200 L, has an input of 7 L/hr of brine of concentration 5 kg/L, and an output of 5 L/hr. Tank 1 pumps 1 L/hr into tank 2, and tank 2 pumps 3 L/hr into tank 1.

Let \(x_i(t)\) be the mass of salt in tank \(i\text{.}\) The statements above imply

\begin{align*} \dd{x_1}{t} \amp = 4\cdot 3 - 6\cdot \frac{x_1}{100} - 1\cdot \frac{x_1}{100} + 3 \cdot \frac{x_2}{200} \\ \dd{x_2}{t} \amp = 7\cdot 5 - 5\cdot \frac{x_2}{200} - 3\cdot \frac{x_2}{200} + 1\cdot \frac{x_1}{100}\text{.} \end{align*}

This is neatly expressed using linear algebra:

\begin{equation*} \dd{}{t} \twovec{x_1}{x_2} = \frac{1}{200}\twomat{-14}{3}{2}{-8} \twovec{x_1}{x_2} + \twovec{12}{35} \text{.} \end{equation*}

Here we have naturally extended the derivative of a vector to mean the derivative of each component.

The model in Example 3.1.1 is of an important general form.

Definition 3.1.2. Linear system of ODEs.

A linear system of ODEs is an equation of the form

\begin{equation} \bx' = \bA(t)\bx + \bff(t)\text{,}\label{fs-eq-system}\tag{3.1.1} \end{equation}

where \(\bx\) is an \(n\)-dimensional state vector, \(\bA\) is an \(n\times n\) coefficient matrix, and \(\bff(t)\) is an \(n\)-dimensional forcing function. If given, an initial condition of the system is a time \(t_0\) and vector \(\bx_0\) such that \(\bx(t_0)=\bx_0\text{.}\)

Because the matrix in Example 3.1.1 is constant in time, we would call its model a constant-coefficient system.

It's not a stretch to say that virtually all of the general statements we made about the scalar linear problem \(x'=a(t)x+f(t)\) can be remade with boldface/capital letters for the linear system \(\bx'=\bA(t)\bx+\bff(t)\text{.}\) Those statements relied mainly on linearity, which remains present. So for example,

Theorem 3.1.3. General solutions (first-order linear systems).

Every solution of \(\bx'=\bA(t)\bx+\bff(t)\) can be written in the form \(\bx=\bx_h+\bx_p\text{,}\) where \(\bx_h\) is the general solution of \(\bx'=\bA(t)\bx\) and \(\bx_p\) is any solution of \(\bx'=\bA(t)\bx+\bff(t)\text{.}\)

Once again, then, we look first at the homogeneous system (no forcing term), and then for particular solutions of the nonhomogeneous problem.
