Similarly, for the forced problem \(my''+ky=e^{i\omega t}\text{,}\) we can get a particular solution \(y_p=Y e^{i\omega t}\) if we let \(Y=1/(k-m\omega^2)\text{.}\) This works for both cosine and sine forcing upon taking real and imaginary parts, and we go right back to the formulas from before.
