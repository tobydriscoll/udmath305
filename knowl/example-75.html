Example 4.6.3.

To solve \(x'' - 4x + 3x = 6\) with \(x(0)=-2\text{,}\) \(x'(0)=4\text{,}\) we take transforms to obtain

\begin{equation*} X(s) = \frac{-2s + 8 + 4}{(s-3)(s-1)} + \frac{6}{s(s-3)(s-1)} . \end{equation*}

The PFD of this has the form

\begin{equation*} X(s) = \frac{A}{s-3} + \frac{B}{s-1} + \frac{C}{s}. \end{equation*}

Equating and clearing the denominators leads to

\begin{equation*} (s)(12-2s) + 6 = As(s-1) + Bs(s-3) + C(s-1)(s-3). \end{equation*}

The values \(s=0,s=1,s=3\text{,}\) which are simply the poles of \(X\text{,}\) leave especially simple equations: \(6=3C\text{,}\) \(10+6=-2B\text{,}\) and \(18+6=6A\text{.}\) Hence

\begin{equation*} X(s) = 4\, \frac{1}{s-3} - 8\, \frac{1}{s-1} + 2\, \frac{1}{s}. \end{equation*}

This inverts easily to give \(x(t) = 4e^{3t} - 8e^t + 2\text{.}\)
