The dot product gives us a new way to describe the matrix-vector product \(\bA\bx\text{.}\) The product \(\by=\bA\bx\) is an \(m\times 1\) vector whose \(i\)th element is the dot product of \(\bx\) with the \(i\)th row of \(\bA\text{.}\) More symbolically,

\begin{equation*} y_i = \sum_{k=1}^n A_{ik} x_k, \qquad i=1,\ldots,m\text{.} \end{equation*}

This is, naturally, equivalent to the earlier definition of \(\by\) as a linear combination of the columns of \(\bA\text{,}\) with the combination coefficients coming from \(\bx\text{.}\)
