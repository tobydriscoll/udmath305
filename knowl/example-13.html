Example 1.6.4.

Suppose we want to check the independence of

\begin{equation*} \bx_1 = \threevec{1}{2}{-1}, \quad \bx_2 = \threevec{2}{4}{-2}, \quad \bx_3 = \threevec{-1}{1}{4}\text{.} \end{equation*}

We assemble these as the columns of a matrix

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \amp -1 \\ 2 \amp 4 \amp 1 \\ -1 \amp -2 \amp 4 \end{bmatrix} \text{.} \end{equation*}

Technically we ought to tack on a zero column to get the augmented matrix, but row operations can never change that column. So we can find the RREF without it:

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 2 \amp -1 \\ 2 \amp 4 \amp 1 \\ -1 \amp -2 \amp 4 \end{bmatrix} \mapsto \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 1 \amp -1 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 3 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 3 \end{bmatrix} \mapsto \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 1 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 1 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus the homogeneous problem will have at least one free parameter.
