The solutions of \(\bx'=\bA(t)\bx\) largely boil down to finding a fundamental matrix, which satisfies

\begin{equation*} \bX' = \bigl[ \bx_1' \: \bx_2' \: \cdots \: \bx_n' \bigr] = \bigl[ \bA\bx_1 \: \bA\bx_2 \: \cdots \: \bA\bx_n \bigr] = \bA \bX\text{.} \end{equation*}

With such a matrix in hand, the general solution is \(\bx(t)=\bX(t)\bc\) for an arbitrary “integration constant” \(\bc\text{.}\)
