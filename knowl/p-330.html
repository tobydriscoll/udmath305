To find an alternative real form, we write

\begin{align*} e^{(-1+i)t} \twovec{1}{2-i} \amp = e^{-t} [\cos(t)+i\sin(t)] \left( \twovec{1}{2} + i\twovec{0}{-1} \right)\\ \amp = e^{-t} \left( \cos(t)\twovec{1}{2} - \sin(t) \twovec{0}{-1} \right)\\ \amp \qquad + i \left( \cos(t)\twovec{0}{-1} + \sin(t) \twovec{1}{2} \right) \text{.} \end{align*}

Thus the general solution can also be written as

\begin{equation*} \bx(t) = b_1 e^{-t} \twovec{\cos(t)}{2\cos(t)+\sin(t)} + b_2 e^{-t}\twovec{\sin(t)}{2\sin(t)-\cos(t)} \text{,} \end{equation*}

where here \(b_1\) and \(b_2\) are real.
