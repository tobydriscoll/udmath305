Example 4.1.2.

Two pendulums hanging from a bar and swinging in parallel planes are coupled through torsion on the bar, according to

\begin{align*} \theta_1''+ b\theta_1' + \frac{g}{L}\sin(\theta_1) + \kappa(\theta_1-\theta_2) \amp = 0 \\ \theta_2''+ b\theta_2' + \frac{g}{L}\sin(\theta_2) + \kappa(\theta_2-\theta_1) \amp = 0 \end{align*}

We define

\begin{equation*} u_1 = \theta_1, \quad u_2 = \theta_1', \quad u_3 = \theta_2, \quad u_4 = \theta_2'. \end{equation*}

Two differential equations, \(u_1'=u_2\) and \(u_3'=u_4\text{,}\) follow automatically from the definitions. The remaining two come from substituting into the original ODEs:

\begin{align*} u_2' = \theta_1'' \amp = - b u_2 - \frac{g}{L}\sin(u_1) - \kappa(u_1-u_3)\\ u_4' = \theta_2'' \amp = - b u_4 - \frac{g}{L}\sin(u_3) - \kappa(u_3-u_1) \end{align*}

The result is a four-dimensional, first-order system \(\bu'=\mathbf{g}(t,\bu)\text{.}\)
