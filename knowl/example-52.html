Example 4.5.3

We find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp -1 \\ 5 \amp -3 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The characteristic polynomial is \(\lambda^2 +2 \lambda +2\text{,}\) with roots \(\lambda_{1,2} = -1 \pm 1i.\)

To find an eigenvector for \(\lambda_1\text{,}\) we use the first row of \(\bA - \lambda_1 \mathbf{I} \) to conclude \((2-i)v_1 -v_2 = 0\text{,}\) so \(v_2=(2-i)v_1\text{.}\) Any multiple of the vector \([1,2-i]^T\) will do.

We get a benefit from the complex eigenvalues: the second eigenvector, like the second eigenvalue, is just the conjugate of the first. So we have \([1,2+i]^T\) (and all its multiples) to go with \(\lambda_2\text{.}\)
