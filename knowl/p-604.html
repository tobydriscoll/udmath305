With \(u=x^{-1}\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} \dd{u}{t} = \dd{u}{x}\,\dd{x}{t} = -x^{-2} \dd{x}{t}. \end{equation*}

From this the original ODE becomes

\begin{align*} -x^2 u' \amp = x + t x^2 \\ u' \amp = -x{-1} - t, \end{align*}

which gives \(u'+u=-t\text{.}\) This has particular solution \(u_p=At+B\text{,}\) and we quickly find \(u_p=1-t\text{.}\) Hence the general solution is \(u = c_1 e^{-t} + 1 - t, \) and therefore

\begin{equation*} x = \frac{1}{c_1 e^{-t} + 1 - t}. \end{equation*}

This is a special case of a Bernoulli equation.
