Solution 3.4.4.3.1.
\begin{align*} \lx[e^{i\omega t}] \amp = \frac{1}{s-i\omega} \\ \lx[\cos(\omega t) + i \sin(\omega t)] \amp = \frac{1}{s-i\omega} \cdot \frac{s+i\omega}{s+i\omega} \\ \lx[\cos(\omega t)] + i \lx[\sin(\omega t)] \amp = \frac{s+i\omega}{s^2 + \omega^2} \end{align*}

By taking real and imaginary parts, obtain

\begin{equation*} \lx[\cos(\omega t)] = \frac{s}{s^2 + \omega^2}, \qquad \lx[\sin(\omega t)] = \frac{\omega}{s^2 + \omega^2} \end{equation*}
