Consider the ODE \(x'=x-x^3\text{.}\) The equilibrium solutions are \(x=0\) and \(x=\pm 1\text{.}\) Consider also that \(x'\lt 0\) if \(-1\lt x \lt 0\) or \(x\gt 1\text{;}\) otherwise, \(x' \gt 0\text{.}\) The phase line diagram clarifies that the steady states at \(x=\pm 1\) are stable, while the one at \(x=0\) is unstable.
