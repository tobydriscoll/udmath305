\begin{equation*} \dd{x}{t} = x \end{equation*}

It's trivial to check that \(x=e^t\) is one solution of \(x'=x\text{,}\) i.e., it makes the ODE a true equation. As a mathematical model, the ODE states that the rate of change in \(x\) is equal to \(x\) itself, and the result is exponential growth: the solution grows larger, which increases the rate of growth, and so on in a feedback loop.

Exponential growth is most naturally plotted on a log-linear graph, in which case the solution is a straight line with positive slope. Because of the log scale, a “difference” in the \(y\)-coordinate is actually a multiplicative factor in the solution. Thus each unit of time applies a constant multiplier of growth.
