To solve \(y'=2ty - t\) with \(y(0)=0\text{,}\) we identify \(a(t)=2t\text{.}\) Hence \(G(s,t)=\exp(t^2-s^2)\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} y(t) = \int_0^t (-s) e^{t^2-s^2}\, ds = \frac{1}{2} \left( 1 - e^{t^2} \right)\text{.} \end{equation*} 
dydt = @(t,y) 2*t*y - t;
[t,y] = ode45(dydt,[0,2],0);
plot(t,y)
xlabel('t'), ylabel('y(t)')
