Why is this the right concept? Well, if the vectors are dependent, then by definition there is some \(j\) for which we can write

\begin{equation*} c_j \bx_j = - \sum_{i\neq j} c_i \bx_i\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(c_j\neq 0\text{.}\) If we divide this through by \(c_j\text{,}\) then we have shown that \(\bx_j\) lies within the span of all the other vectors, and therefore including it with linear combinations is redundant.
