We can find the eigenstuff of

\begin{equation*} \bA = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp 1 \\ 4 \amp 1 \end{bmatrix} \end{equation*}

as \(\lambda_1=3\text{,}\) \(\bv_1 = \twovec{1}{2}\text{,}\) and \(\lambda_2=-1\text{,}\) \(\bv_2=\twovec{1}{-2}\text{.}\) This gives us solutions of \(\bx'=\bA\bx\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} \bx_1(t) = e^{3t} \twovec{1}{2},\quad \bx_2(t) = e^{-t} \twovec{1}{-2} \text{.} \end{equation*}

Because the eigenvalues are distinct, we know that these are independent solutions. The general homogeneous solution is

\begin{equation*} \bx(t) = \bX(t)\bc = c_1 e^{3t} \twovec{1}{2} + c_2 e^{-t} \twovec{1}{-2} = \twovec{ c_1 e^{3t} + c_2 e^{-t}}{2c_1 e^{3t} - 2 c_2e^{-t}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
