The characteristic roots are negative and real, so all solutions are of the form \(y=c_1 e^{s_1t} + c_2 e^{s_2 t}\text{,}\) where \(s_2\lt s_1 \lt 0\text{.}\) As time advances, \(e^{s_2 t}\) decays more rapidly than the other exponential, so \(y\approx c_1 e^{s_1 t}\) and \(y'\approx c_1 s_1 e^{s_1 t}\text{.}\) That is, solutions in the phase plane asymptotically approach the origin parallel to the vector \((1,s_1)\text{.}\) In this situation we say that the origin is a node of the ODE.
