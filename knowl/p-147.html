The analogy goes even deeper. Recall in SubsectionÂ 1.4.3 that the null solution was relatable to the impulse response, i.e., the particular solution when \(f(t)=\delta(t)\text{.}\) That happens here, too. In fact, the solution of \(my''+ky=\delta(t)\) with zero initial conditions is

\begin{equation} g(t) = \frac{1}{m\omega_n} \sin(\omega_n t).\tag{2.1.2} \end{equation}

This solution has \(g(0)=0\text{,}\) \(g'(0)=1/m\text{,}\) and solves \(my''+ky=0\) for \(t\gt 0\text{.}\)
