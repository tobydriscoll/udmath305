Finally, consider the driven, undamped oscillator \(x''+\omega_0^2 x = \cos(\omega t)\text{.}\) It leads to

\begin{equation*} X_p(s) = \frac{s}{(s^2+\omega_0^2)(s^2+\omega^2)} = \frac{1}{\omega_0^2-\omega^2} \left( \frac{s}{s^2+\omega^2} - \frac{s}{s^2+\omega_0^2} \right)\text{,} \end{equation*}

which is readily inverted into

\begin{equation*} x_p(t) = \frac{1}{\omega_0^2-\omega^2} \bigl[ \cos(\omega t) - \cos(\omega_0 t) \bigr]. \end{equation*}

That is a particular solution, but unlike the method of undetermined coefficients we used before, it includes \(\cos(\omega_0 t)\text{,}\) which comes from the homogeneous solution. In the Laplace transform method, we set the initial conditions to zero in order to simplify the algebra, and in general that means the homogeneous terms are needed in order to satisfy them. Undetermined coefficients pays no attention to initial conditions and can therefore ignore the homogeneous terms.
