We can write a complex number \(z\in \mathbb{C}\) as \(z=x+iy\text{,}\) where \(i^2=-1\) and \(x\) and \(y\) are real numbers known as the real and imaginary parts of \(z\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} z = x+iy \quad \Leftrightarrow \quad x = \Re(z), \quad y = \Im(z)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Writing a complex number this way is equivalent to using rectangular or Cartesian coordinates in the plane to specify a point \((x,y)\text{.}\) Thus complex numbers are on a “number plane” rather than a number line.
