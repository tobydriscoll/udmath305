Continuing the underdamped case, the gain works out to be

\begin{equation*} G^2 = \frac{1}{(\omega_n^2-\omega^2)^2 + 4\omega_n^2Z^2\omega^2} = \frac{\omega_n^{-4}}{(1-\rho^2)^2 + 4Z^2\rho^2}\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(\rho=\omega/\omega_n\) is a dimensionless frequency ratio. If also \(Z^2\lt 1/2\text{,}\) the gain is maximized when \(\rho=\sqrt{1-2Z^2} \lt 1\text{.}\) The gain at that maximum is proportional to \(1/Z\text{,}\) so much growth in the response is possible near the natural frequency when damping is small in the sense of \(Z\approx 0\text{.}\) This can be considered a practical resonance for lightly damped systems.
