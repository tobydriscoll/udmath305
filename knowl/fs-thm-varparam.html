Theorem 3.8.1. Variation of parameters.

Let \(\bX(t)\) be a fundamental matrix of the homogeneous problem \(\bx'=\bA(t)\bx\text{.}\) Then the general solution of \(\bx'=\bA(t)\bx+\bf(t)\) is

\begin{equation} \bx(t) = \bx_h(t) + \bx_p(t) = \bX(t)\bc + \bX(t) \int \bX(t)^{-1} \bff(t) \, dt\text{.}\tag{3.8.1} \end{equation}

The solution with initial condition \(\bx(0)=\bx_0\) is

\begin{equation} \bx(t) = \bX(t)\bX(0)^{-1}\bx_0 + \bX(t) \int_0^t \bX(s)^{-1}\bff(s) \, ds\text{.}\tag{3.8.2} \end{equation}

In the case of a constant matrix \(\bA\text{,}\) one option is \(\bX(t)=e^{t\bA}, and\)

\begin{equation} \bx(t) = e^{t\bA}\bx_0 + \int_0^t e^{(t-s)\bA} \bff(s) \, ds\text{.}\tag{3.8.3} \end{equation}
