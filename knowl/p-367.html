Our main item of business is the linear, constant-coefficient model problem

\begin{equation} x'' + bx' + cx = f(t)\text{,}\tag{4.1.1} \end{equation}

for which the conversion process with \(u_1=x\text{,}\) \(u_2=x'\) yields

\begin{equation} \twovec{u_1'}{u_2'} = \twovec{u_2}{f(t)-cx_1-bx_2} = \twomat{0}{1}{-c}{-b} \bu + \twovec{0}{f(t)}\text{,}\tag{4.1.2} \end{equation}

which has the form \(\bu'=\bA\bu+\bff(t)\text{.}\) Thus everything from the last chapter is again in play.
