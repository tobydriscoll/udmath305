Next consider a driven oscillator,

\begin{equation*} m \ddd{y}{t} + b \dd{y}{t} + k y = A \cos(\omega t)\text{.} \end{equation*}

We again use new variables defined by

\begin{equation*} t = \tau T, \quad y = \eta Y, \end{equation*}

with dimensionless \(T\) and \(Y\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \frac{m\eta}{\tau^2} \ddd{Y}{T} + \frac{b\eta}{\tau} \dd{Y}{T} + k \eta Y = A \cos(\omega \tau T)\text{,} \end{equation*}

thus

\begin{equation*} \ddd{Y}{T} + \frac{b\tau}{m} \dd{Y}{T} + \frac{k\tau^2}{m} Y = \frac{A\tau^2}{m\eta} \cos(\omega \tau T)\text{.} \end{equation*}

With only \(\tau\) and \(\eta\) at our disposal, we can't simplify both the first-order and zeroth-order coefficients. We choose

\begin{equation*} \frac{k\tau^2}{m} = 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \tau = \sqrt{\frac{m}{k}} = \frac{1}{\omega_0}\text{,} \end{equation*}

using our old definition of natural frequency. We also use

\begin{equation*} \frac{A\tau^2}{m\eta} = 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad \eta = \frac{A\tau^2}{m} = \frac{A}{k}\text{.} \end{equation*}

So now

\begin{equation*} \ddd{Y}{T} + \frac{b}{k\tau} \dd{Y}{T} + Y = \cos(\omega T/\omega_0)\text{.} \end{equation*}

The coefficient of the first-order term is dimensionless. We might as well simplify a bit more via

\begin{equation*} 2\zeta = \frac{b}{k\tau} = \frac{b}{\sqrt{mk}} \text{.} \end{equation*}

That way, the characteristic equation is just \(s^2+2\zeta s + 1\text{,}\) with roots \(-\zeta\pm\sqrt{\zeta^2-1}\text{.}\) Instead of having to describe what happens in terms of the original \(k,m,b\) trio, we only need worry about whether \(\zeta\) is less than, equal to, or greater than one.
