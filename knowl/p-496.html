For \(0\lt Z \lt 1\) the eigenvalues of (4.4.1) are complex:

\begin{equation*} \lambda_{1,2} = -Z \omega_0 \pm i \omega_0 \sqrt{1-Z^2}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Defining \(\omega_d=\omega_0 \sqrt{1-Z^2}\text{,}\) the general homogeneous solution is therefore

\begin{equation*} x_h(t) = e^{-Z\omega_0 t} \left( c_1 e^{i\omega_d t} + c_2 e^{-i\omega_d t} \right)\text{,} \end{equation*}

or the equivalent real form with cos and sin. This solution is pseudoperiodic, combining oscillation at frequency \(\omega_d \lt \omega_0\) inside an exponential decay envelope. This situation is called an underdamped oscillator. As in the undamped case, we have three different ways to express the real unforced solution in the underdamped case, corresponding to (4.2.1), (4.2.2), and (4.2.3).
