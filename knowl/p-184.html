The easiest ODE in the world is

\begin{equation*} \dd{x}{t} = 0\text{.} \end{equation*}

A solution of this ODE is a function \(x(t)\) that satisfies the ODE. Perhaps you can work out that the solution here is \(x(t)=C\) for an arbitrary constant \(C\text{.}\) As dumb as this example is, it points out something important right away.
