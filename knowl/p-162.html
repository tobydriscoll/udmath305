In the \(2\times 2\) case, suppose \(\lambda\) is already known to be an eigenvalue. Let the first row of \(\bA-\lambda\bI\) be \([\alpha,\beta]\text{.}\) If \(\alpha=\beta=0\text{,}\) then the eigenspace is all of \(\Cn{2}\text{.}\) Otherwise, the eigenspace is \(\span\left(\twovec{-\beta}{\alpha}\right)\text{.}\)
