We find fixed points when \(3x-(xy/2)=(xy/4)-y=0\text{,}\) which has two solutions, \((0,0)\) and \((4,6)\text{.}\) The Jacobian matrix of the system is

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{A}(x,y) = \begin{bmatrix} F_x \amp F_y \\ G_x \amp G_y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3-y/2 \amp -x/2 \\ y/4 \amp -1+x/4 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

At the origin, the Jacobian becomes

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{A}(0,0) = \begin{bmatrix} 3 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp -1 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The eigenvalues of a diagonal matrix (in fact, a triangular matrix) are the diagonal entries. Hence the origin is a saddle point.
