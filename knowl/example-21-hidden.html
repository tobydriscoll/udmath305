Suppose \(ty' = y-ty\text{.}\) Nothing happens until you see that you can factor out \(y\) on the right side. Then we have \(dy/dt = y(1-t)/t\text{,}\) or

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{y} = (t^{-1}-1)\,dt\text{.} \end{equation*}

Thus \(\ln|y| = \ln|t|-t+C\text{,}\) or \(y=A te^{-t}\text{.}\)

Note that this problem is also linear, so it could be approached that way as well. Of course you must get the same solution in the end!
