Counting algebraic multiplicities, we know that \(\bA\) has eigenvalues \(\lambda_1,\ldots,\lambda_n\text{.}\) Say that we have eigenvectors \(\bv_1,\ldots,\bv_n\) to go with them. Then we have \(n\) homogeneous solutions

\begin{equation*} \bx_j(t) = e^{\lambda_j t} \bv_j, \quad j=1,\ldots,n\text{.} \end{equation*}

To determine whether they form a fundamental matrix, we need to determine whether they form a basis. According to TheoremÂ 3.2.3, we can ask that question at any value of \(t\text{,}\) including \(t=0\text{.}\) So the key issue is the independence of the eigenvectors.
