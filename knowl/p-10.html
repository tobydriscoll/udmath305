If we look at the equations

\begin{align*} x - 3y \amp = 1 \\ -2x + 6y \amp = 2 \text{,} \end{align*}

then we identify \((a,b,c,d)=(1,-3,-2,6)\text{,}\) and \(ad-bc=6-6=0\text{.}\) So we know there is no unique solution (parallel lines). Dividing the second equation by \(-2\) leads to the equivalent system

\begin{align*} x - 3y \amp = 1 \\ x - 3y \amp = -1 \text{.} \end{align*}

It's clear that there is no way to satisfy both equations simultaneously.
