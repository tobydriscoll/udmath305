A pendulum is governed by the nonlinear equation \(\theta''+ b\theta' + \frac{g}{L}\sin(\theta)=0\text{,}\) where \(\theta(t)\) is the angle made by the pendulum from the straight-down position. Define \(u_1=\theta\text{,}\) \(u_2=\theta'\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \twovec{u_1'}{u_2'} = \twovec{u_2}{-bu_2 - \frac{g}{L}\sin(u_1)} \end{equation*}

is an equivalent system.
