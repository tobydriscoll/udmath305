This is a linear PDE, and we expect that we a linear combination of all the possible solutions is also a solution:

\begin{equation*} u(t,x) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n T_n(t)X_n(x) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n e^{-\alpha^2 n^2 t} \sin(n\pi x)\text{.} \end{equation*}

(We absorbed the initial \(T\) values into the coefficients.) The final piece of the puzzle is the initial condition. I hope you're sitting down for this, because

\begin{equation*} f(x) = u(0,x) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n \sin(n\pi x)\text{.} \end{equation*}

We have a forking Fourier sine series here, people!
