As we have already seen, a mass hanging from a spring satisfies the ODE

\begin{equation*} my'' + b y' + ky = f(t)\text{.} \end{equation*}

(As is typical, all the named constants are nonnegative.) Here \(m\)> is the mass, \(b\)>is a coefficient of friction or other damping forces, and \(k\)> is a characteristic constant of the spring. The term \(f(t)\) represents an external driving force. If we have initial conditions, they give values for \(y\) and \(y'\text{.}\)
