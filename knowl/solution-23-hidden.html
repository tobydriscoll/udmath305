The chain rule gives \(\phi'=-\theta'\) and \(\phi''=\theta''\text{.}\) The ODE follows from the identity \(\sin(\pi-\phi)=\sin(\phi)\text{.}\) The eigenvalues from the characteristic equation are

\begin{equation*} \frac{-\gamma \pm \sqrt{\gamma^2 + 4(g/L)}}{2}. \end{equation*}

The radical gives a real value greater than \(\gamma\text{,}\) so the plus sign gives a positive real eigenvalue and the minus sign gives a negative one. (If you pull the pendulum a little away from the top and push it just right, it will gently return to the inverted position, in theory!)
