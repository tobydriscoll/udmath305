Example 1.4.1

To solve \(y'+3y=7\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} y(t) = e^{-3t}y(0) + 7 \int_0^t e^{-3(t-s)} \, ds = e^{-3t}y(0) + \frac{7}{3}( 1 - e^{-3t})\text{.} \end{equation*}

From here it's immediate to see that \(y\to 7/3\) as \(t\to\infty\text{.}\)

dydt = @(t,y) 7-3*y;
[t,y] = ode45(dydt,[0,5],-1);
plot(t,y)
xlabel('t'), ylabel('y(t)')
title('Constant forcing')
