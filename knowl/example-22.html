Example 1.11.10.

We find the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of

\begin{equation*} \begin{bmatrix} 1 \amp -1 \\ 5 \amp -3 \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The characteristic polynomial is \(\lambda^2 +2 \lambda +2\text{,}\) with roots \(\lambda_{1,2} = -1 \pm 1i.\)

To find an eigenvector for \(\lambda_1\text{,}\) we note that the first row of \(\bA - \lambda_1 \mathbf{I} \) is \([(2-i),-1]\text{.}\) Thus a basis for this eigenspace is the single eigenvector \(\twovec{1}{2-i}\text{.}\)

We get a benefit here from the complex eigenvalues: the conjugate of an eigenvector for \(\lambda_1\) will be an eigenvector for \(\lambda_2\text{.}\) So we have \(\spanset\left(\twovec{1}{2+i}\right)\) to go with \(\lambda_2\text{.}\)
