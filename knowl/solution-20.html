Solution 3.6.3.3.1.

Both \(\mathbf{U}\) and \(\mathbf{L}\) are defective with zero eigenvalues. However, \(\mathbf{U}^2=\mathbf{L}^2=\bzero\text{,}\) so

\begin{align*} e^{t\mathbf{U}} \amp = \twomat{1}{-t}{0}{1}, \\ e^{t\mathbf{L}} \amp = \twomat{1}{0}{t}{1}. \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{equation*} e^{t\mathbf{L}}\,e^{t\mathbf{U}} = \twomat{1}{-t}{t}{1-t^2}\text{,} \end{equation*}

which is nothing like the rotation matrix from the previous exercise.
