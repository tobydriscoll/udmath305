Example 2.8.1.

Let's solve \(x'-x=f(t)\text{,}\) where \(f(t)\) is 5 for \(3 \lt t \le 6\) and zero elsewhere. We have \(a=1\text{.}\) The particular solution due to the first step is

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{1} 5 H(t-3) \bigl[e^{(3-t)}-1\bigr]\text{,} \end{equation*}

and from the second step it's

\begin{equation*} -\frac{1}{1} 5 H(t-6) \bigl[e^{(6-t)}-1\bigr]\text{.} \end{equation*}

Put these together with the homogeneous solution and we get

\begin{equation*} x(t) = e^{t}x_0 + 5 H(t-3) \bigl[e^{(3-t)}-1\bigr] - 5 H(t-6) \bigl[e^{(6-t)}-1\bigr]\text{.} \end{equation*} 
step = @(t) double(t>0);
dydt = @(t,y) y + 5*(step(t-3) - step(t-6));
[t,y] = ode45(dydt,[0,8],1);
plot(t,y)
xlabel('t'), ylabel('y(t)')
title('Window forcing')
