The first case for (4.4.1) is \(Z=0\text{,}\) which is the idealization of no mechanical damping or friction (or resistance, in a circuit). The homogeneous equation is \(x''+\omega_0^2x=0\text{,}\) which has eigenvalues \(\pm i\omega_0\) and a general solution that can be expressed three ways:

\begin{align*} x_h(t) \amp = \Re \left[ c_1 e^{i\omega_0 t} \right] \\ \amp = a_1 \cos(\omega_0 t) + a_2 \sin(\omega_0 t) \\ \amp = R \cos(\omega_0 t - \theta) \end{align*}

Thus the undamped case results in pure oscillation at frequency \(\omega_0\text{.}\) This is known as simple harmonic motion.
