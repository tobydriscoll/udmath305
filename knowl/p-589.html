Scalar, first-order nonlinear problems are often more penetrable than more general equations. If nothing else, the problem

\begin{equation*} x' = f(t,x) \end{equation*}

is easily visualized. By evaluating \(f\) on a grid in the \((t,x)\) plane, we can draw arrows representing the slope \(dx/dt\) of the solution curves that pass through the grid points. This picture is known as a direction field.
