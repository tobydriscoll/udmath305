The kicker is that the impulse response can be used to solve any forcing problem, at least in principle. Given the forcing term \(f(t)\text{,}\) a particular solution is

\begin{equation*} y_p(t) = \int_0^t g(t-s)f(s)\,ds. \end{equation*}

Again, in a linear problem we just need to add up the contributions of impulses at every moment. Because of this formula, the impulse response \(g\) is also called a fundamental solution. We'll be using and coming at this formula a few different ways throughout the chapter.
