Example 1.10.1

Consider the variable growth archetypeÂ 1.1.3 \(y'=2ty\text{.}\) We express \(y'\) as \(dy/dt\) and then isolate the variables:

\begin{equation*} \frac{dy}{y} = 2t\,dt\text{.} \end{equation*}

Integrating both sides leads to \(\log |y| = t^2 + C\text{,}\) or \(|y|=Ae^{t^2}\) for a positive constant \(A\) (since it is the exponential of a real constant). Taking the absolute value off of \(y\) means that \(A\) can be negative as well. Also, \(A=0\) clearly leads to a solution. Finally, then, \(y=Ce^{t^2}\) for arbitrary \(C\text{.}\)
