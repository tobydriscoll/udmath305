Euler's identity (3.4.1) gives us an alternative to the rectangular decomposition \(z=u+iv\text{.}\) If \(r\) is a positive number, and we write

\begin{equation*} re^{i\theta} = (r\cos \theta)+i(r\sin \theta)\text{,} \end{equation*}

then it is clear that \((r,\theta)\) represent the polar coordinates of the point. Furthermore, the polar \(r\) is just the modulus \(|z|\text{.}\) (The angle \(\theta\) might variously be called the argument or phase of \(z\text{.}\))
