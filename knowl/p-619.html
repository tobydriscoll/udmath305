The logistic model posits a linear decrease in the per capita growth rate as the population increases:

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{x} \, \dd{x}{t} = a\left(1 - \frac{x}{K}\right) \text{.} \end{equation*}

But there are many cases where we might have an even faster decrease in the growth rate. One such case is the Gompertz equation,

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{x} \, \dd{x}{t} = a\left(1 - \frac{\log(x)}{\log(K)}\right) \text{.} \end{equation*}

It has been suggested as a model for the growth of tumors. Show that the solution is

\begin{equation*} x = K \exp\left[ -c_1 \exp \Bigl( - at/\log(K) \Bigr) \right], \end{equation*}

for a nonnegative \(c_1\text{.}\)
