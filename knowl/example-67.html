Example 5.1.1

You can verify quickly that \(\twovec{2}{-1}\) and \(\twovec{1}{2}\) are orthogonal in 2-D. Then we can verify, for instance,

\begin{equation*} \twovec{3}{5} = \frac{6-5}{2^2+(-1)^2} \twovec{2}{-1} + \frac{3+10}{2^2+1^2} \twovec{1}{2} = \frac{1}{5} \twovec{2+13}{-1+26}\text{.} \end{equation*}
