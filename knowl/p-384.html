There is a key connection between the rectangular form of a complex number and the polar form of its exponential:

\begin{equation} e^{(a+i \omega)t} = e^{at} e^{i \omega t} = e^{at} (\cos \omega t + i \sin \omega t)\text{.}\tag{3.4.2} \end{equation}

The first term in the product is the familiar real \(e^{at}\text{,}\) which is an amplitude that exponentially grows or decays in time (or stays fixed if \(a=0\)). The other term has modulus one and can be interpreted as a parameterization of the unit circle in the complex plane with constant angular velocity \(\omega\text{.}\)
