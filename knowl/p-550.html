We can use a simple trick to create direction fields for the nonautonomous scalar problem \(x'=f(t,x)\) as well. Introducing the variables \(u_1=t\) and \(u_2=x\text{,}\) we have

\begin{align*} \dd{u_1}{t} \amp = 1, \\ \dd{u_2}{t} \amp = f(u_1,u_2), \end{align*}

which has the form of a two-dimensional autonomous system.
