We begin with the steady states. We have \(x'=f(x)=x(a-bx)\) with roots \(x=0\) and \(x=a/b\text{.}\) Since \(f'(x)=a-2bx\text{,}\) we conclude that the former steady state is unstable and the latter is stable. The stable equilibrium value \(K=a/b\) is important and known as the carrying capacity of the environment. We will find later that this value is the long-term fate of the system for any positive initial condition.
