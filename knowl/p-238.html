To find a particular solution of \(x'+3x=6t-4e^{-t}\text{,}\) we can separately find them for the cases \(x'+3x=6t\) and \(x'+3x=-4e^{-t}\text{.}\) Above we found \(x_p=2t-(2/3)\) for the first case. For the second case we choose \(x_p=A e^{-t}\) and plug in:

\begin{equation*} -4e^{-t} = x'+3x = -A e^{-t} + 3A e^{-t}\text{,} \end{equation*}

which tells us that \(A=-2\text{.}\) Finally, the particular solution of the original equation is

\begin{equation*} x_p(t) = 2t-\frac{2}{3}-2e^{-t}\text{.} \end{equation*}
