Suppose we chain together two first-order equations, i.e,

\begin{equation*} x_1'-r_1x_1 = f, \qquad x_2' - r_2x_2=x_1\text{,} \end{equation*}

with \(x_1(0)=x_2(0)=0\text{.}\) Since

\begin{equation*} X_2(s)=G_2(s)X_1(s)=G_2(s)G_1(s)F(s)\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(G_1,G_2\) are the transfer functions of the individual operators, then \(G_2(s)G_1(s)\) serves as a transfer function for the chain. Show without transforms that the ODE chain is indeed equivalent to a 2nd-order equation \(\opA[x_2]=f\text{,}\) where \(\opA\) has eigenvalues \(r_1\) and \(r_2\text{.}\) Then show that the same is true for the chain

\begin{equation*} x_1'-r_2x_1 = f, \qquad x_2' - r_1x_2=x_1\text{.} \end{equation*}
