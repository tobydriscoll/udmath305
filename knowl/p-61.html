It's pretty immediate that for any square matrix \(\bA\text{,}\) \(\bA\boldsymbol{0}=\boldsymbol{0}\bA=\boldsymbol{0}\) for the all-zero square matrix \(\boldsymbol{0}\) of appropriate size. So we have a multiplicative zero. But the situation is a bit less obvious when it comes to a multiplicative unit element (the counterpart of the scalar 1).
