Exercise 3. Noncommutative pair.

Calculate \(e^\mathbf{L}\) and \(e^\mathbf{U}\) if

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{U} = \twomat{0}{-1}{0}{0}, \qquad \mathbf{L} = \twomat{0}{0}{1}{0}. \end{equation*}

Then show that

\begin{equation*} e^{t(\mathbf{L}+\mathbf{U})} \neq e^{t\mathbf{L}}\,e^{t\mathbf{U}}. \end{equation*}

(The result of the “rotation matrix” exercise can provide the left-hand side.) This happens because \(\mathbf{L}\mathbf{U}\neq \mathbf{U}\mathbf{L}\text{.}\)
