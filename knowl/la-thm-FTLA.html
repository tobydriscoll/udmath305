Theorem 1.9.1. Fundamental Theorem of Linear Algebra.

Suppose \(\bA\bx=\bb\) is a linear system with an \(n\times n\) matrix \(\bA\text{.}\) Each of the following statements is equivalent to all of the others:

  1. \(\bA\bx=\bb\) has a unique solution,
  2. the linear system has no free variables,
  3. the RREF of \(\bA\) is the \(n\times n\) identity matrix,
  4. \(\bA\) is invertible,
  5. the columns of \(\bA\) are a basis of \(\Rn{n}\text{,}\)
  6. the dimension of \(\null(\bA)\) is zero,
  7. \(\det(\bA)\neq 0\text{,}\) and
  8. \(\bA\) does not have a zero eigenvalue.
