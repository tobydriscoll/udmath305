Although we have only examined two two-dimensional cases, they contain most of what we will see in general. A system of \(n\) linear equations in \(n\) variables with a particular matrix \(\bA\) will either have a unique solution for every choice of \(\bb\text{,}\) or it will have the possibility of no solution or infinitely many solutions depending on \(\bb\text{.}\) In the latter case we say the matrix \(\bA\) is singular, and in the former case we say it is nonsingular. (The term "invertible" is a synonym of nonsingular.)
