The pain is lessened if we use a different form of the general solution by writing out the real and imaginary parts of \(e^{\lambda_1 t} \bv_1\text{.}\) If \(\lambda_1=a+i\omega\text{,}\) then write

\begin{equation*} e^{\lambda_1 t} \bv_1 = e^{at} \bigl[\cos(\omegta t)+i\sin(\omega t) \bigr] \bv_1 = e^{at} \bigl[ u(t) + i w(t) \bigr]\text{.} \end{equation*}

Then an alternative expression for the general solution is \(\bx(t)=b_1 e^{at} u(t) + b_2 e^{at} w(t)\text{,}\) where everything in that formula is now real-valued.
