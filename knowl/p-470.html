It's remarkable that we can generalize the inner product from finite vectors to functions. As usual, summation turns into integration. Let's restrict attention for now to functions on the domain \([-\pi,\pi]\text{.}\) Then we define the inner product of two functions \(u(x),v(x)\) as

\begin{equation*} \inprod{u}{v} = \int_{-\pi}^\pi \overline{u(x)} v(x)\, dx\text{.} \end{equation*}

This definition turns immediately into strange-sounding but mathematically meaningful notions of the "length" (norm) of a function and the angle between two functions.
