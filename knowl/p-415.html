Suppose \(\mathbf{y}'=\mathbf{A} \mathbf{y}\text{.}\) Given a diagonalization of \(\bA\text{,}\) we compute

\begin{align*} \mathbf{y}'\amp =\mathbf{V} \mathbf{D} \mathbf{V}^{-1} \mathbf{y}\\ \mathbf{V}^{-1} \mathbf{y}' \amp=\mathbf{D} \mathbf{V}^{-1} \mathbf{y}\\ (\mathbf{V}^{-1} \mathbf{y})' \amp=\mathbf{D} (\mathbf{V}^{-1} \mathbf{y})\text{.} \end{align*}

This suggests a change of variable, \(\mathbf{u} = \mathbf{V}^{-1} \mathbf{y}.\) Then

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{u}' = \mathbf{D} \mathbf{u} = \begin{bmatrix} \lambda_1 u_1 \\ \lambda_2 u_2 \\ \vdots \\ \lambda_n u_n \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

In terms of the new variable, the ODE system is \(u_1'=\lambda_1 u_1,\, \dots,\, u_n'=\lambda_n u_n\text{,}\) which are completely decoupledâ€“that is, they evolve independently.
