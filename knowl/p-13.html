We will soon be writing a linear system of equations as simply \(\bA\bx=\bb\text{.}\) And just as above, there is a property of \(\bA\) that determines whether or not there is a unique solution. If not, then it depends on both \(\bA\) and \(\bb\) whether there are infinitely many solutions, or none. Those are the only possibilities.
