To find the general solution of \(x''+x=5H(t-4)\) starting from rest, we take transforms to obtain

\begin{equation*} X(s) = \frac{5e^{-4s}}{s(s^2+1)} = 5e^{-4s}F(s) \text{,} \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} F(s) = \frac{1}{s(s^2+1)} = \frac{A}{s} + \frac{Bs+C}{s^2+1}. \end{equation*}

Clearing denominators we get

\begin{equation*} 1 = A(s^2+1) + (Bs+C)(s). \end{equation*}

Setting \(s=0\) gives us \(A=1\) right away. Then \(-s^2=Bs^2+Cs\text{,}\) which tells us that \(B=-1\) and \(C=0\text{.}\) Hence

\begin{equation*} f(t) = 1 -\cos(t). \end{equation*}

So by the shift theorem, the solution is

\begin{equation*} x(t) = 5H(t-4)f(t-4) = 5 H(t-4)[1-\cos(t-4)]. \end{equation*}
