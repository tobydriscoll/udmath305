The ODE is, as before, \(x'' + 1.6 x' + 2.2 x = 0\text{.}\) The eigenvalues are the roots of \(\lambda^2 + 1.6\lambda + 2.2\text{,}\) which are found numerically to be

\begin{equation*} \lambda \approx -0.8000 \pm 1.2490i. \end{equation*}

Note that the imaginary part is smaller than the natural frequency found in the last example, as it must be. From here I will treat these rounded values as though they are exact. Choosing the sin-cos form of the general solution, we have

\begin{align*} x(t) \amp = a_1 e^{-0.8 t} \cos(1.249 t) + a_2 e^{-0.8 t} \sin(1.249 t). \end{align*}

(It is not necessary to use the general formula derived above for this; the solution comes entirely from the eigenvalues.) We apply the initial conditions \(x(0)=0\text{,}\) \(x'(0)=-0.5\) to find

\begin{align*} 0 \amp = x(0) = a_1, \\ -0.5 \amp = x'(0) = a_1( -0.8 ) + a_2 (1.249 ), \end{align*}

thus \(a_2 = -0.4003\text{.}\) The motion is therefore given by \(x(t)=-0.4003\, e^{-0.8 t} \sin(1.249 t)\text{.}\)
