First-order ODEs are often used to model situations of growth and decay. The linear problem \(y'=ay+q(t)\) is a prototype for many important problems:

Population

Population \(y(t)\) of organisms has a constant net per capita birth (or death) rate

Interest

Money amount \(y(t)\) grows at fixed positive interest rate \(a\)

Radioactivity

\(y(t)\) is the mass of a radioactive isotope, and \(a \lt 0\)

Pharmacokinetics

\(y(t)\) is the amount of a drug being metabolized in the body

Newtonian cooling

If \(y(t)\) is the temperature of a body kept in an environment at fixed temperature \(E\text{,}\) then the difference \(z(t)=y(t)-E\) satisfies \(z'=a z\) for a cooling rate \(a \lt 0\)

To belabor the obvious, positive \(a\) represents growth and negative \(a\) leads to decay. In the simplest contexts, the rate \(a\) is constant, but in most models a variable rate is more realistic.
