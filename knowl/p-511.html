Let's solve \(x''-x=24 e^{3t}\text{,}\) with \(x(0)=4\text{,}\) \(x'(0)=12\text{.}\) Transforming both sides gives

\begin{equation*} [s^2X(s) - 4s - 12] - X(s) = \frac{24}{s-3}\text{.} \end{equation*}

We easily solve for \(X\text{:}\)

\begin{equation*} X(s) = \frac{4s+12}{(s^2-1)} + \frac{24}{(s^2-1)(s-3)} \text{.} \end{equation*}

In order to avoid multiple uses of partial fractions, we combine everything into a single fraction:

\begin{equation*} X(s) = \frac{4s^2-12}{(s-1)(s+1)(s-3)} \end{equation*}

The PFD takes the form

\begin{equation*} \frac{4s^2-12}{(s-1)(s+1)(s-3)} = \frac{A}{s-1} + \frac{B}{s+1} + \frac{C}{s-3}, \end{equation*}

which is the same as the identity

\begin{equation*} 4s^2-12 = A(s+1)(s-3) + B(s-1)(s-3) + C(s-1)(s+1). \end{equation*}

One way to handle this is to expand the right-hand side and equate the powers of \(s\text{.}\) In this case it's easier to choose three strategic values of \(s\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} s = 1 \amp \:\Rightarrow\: -8 = -4A \\ s = -1 \amp\: \Rightarrow\: -8 = 8B \\ s = 3 \amp\: \Rightarrow\: 24 = 8C \text{,} \end{align*}

so that

\begin{equation*} X(s) = \frac{2}{s-1} + \frac{-1}{s+1} + \frac{3}{s-3}. \end{equation*}

Now it's clear that \(x(t) = 2e^t - e^{-t} + 3e^{3t}\text{.}\)
