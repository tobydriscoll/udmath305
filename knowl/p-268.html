A more sophisticated model for the cooling of coffee in a well-insulated mug is

\begin{equation*} z' = -rSz, \end{equation*}

where \(z(t)\) is the difference between the coffee temperature and the room temperature, \(S\) is the exposed surface area of the coffee, and \(r\) is a positive rate constant. Thus, a wider mug that exposes more surface area will cause the coffee to cool faster.
