Consider the ODE \(x'=x-x^3\text{.}\) The equilibrium solutions are \(x=0\) and \(x=\pm 1\text{,}\) which is clear in a plot of \(f(x)\text{.}\) The plot also shows the sign of \(f(x)\) and therefore of \(dx/dt\text{.}\) So between the equilibria we can draw arrows to show whether \(x\) is increasing or decreasing with time. The arrows clarify that the steady states at \(x=\pm 1\) are stable, while the one at \(x=0\) is unstable.
