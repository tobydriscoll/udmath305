Of course, a different vector on the right-hand side such as \(\bb=\twovec{-3}{6}\) does lie on this line, and we can solve the system in this case. In fact, there are infinitely many ways to do it:

\begin{equation*} \twovec{-3}{6} = -3 \twovec{1}{-2} = \twovec{1}{-2} - 2\twovec{2}{-4} = \cdots. \end{equation*}

In this situation, the corresponding row picture still has two parallel lines, but they conincide, indicating the infinite set of solutions.
