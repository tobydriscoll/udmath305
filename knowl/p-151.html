Recall in SubsectionÂ 1.4.3 that in first-order problems, the particular solution when \(f(t)=\delta(t)\) is called the impulse response. The solution of \(my''+ky=\delta(t)\) with zero initial conditions is

\begin{equation} g(t) = \frac{1}{m\omega_n} \sin(\omega_n t)\text{.}\tag{2.1.2} \end{equation}

Also like the first-order case, the impulse response is also the solution to a certain homogeneous problem: \(g(0)=0\text{,}\) \(g'(0)=1/m\text{,}\) and solves \(mg''+kg=0\) for \(t\gt 0\text{.}\)
