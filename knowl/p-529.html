Why does this matter? Consider finding a particular solution of \(x''+bx'+cx=f(t)\text{.}\) After taking transforms we get

\begin{equation*} X_p(s) = \frac{1}{s^2+bs+c} F(s) = G(s) F(s)\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(G(s)\) is the transfer function. Right away then, we know that \(x_p(t)=f(t)*g(t)\) is a particular solution.
