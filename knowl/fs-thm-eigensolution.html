Theorem 3.3.1. Solution by eigenvectors.

Let \(\bA\) have eigenvalues \(\lambda_1,\ldots,\lambda_n\) and corresponding eigenvectors \(\bv_1,\ldots,\bv_n\text{.}\) If the eigenvectors are independent (that is, they form a basis), then

\begin{equation} \bX(t) = \begin{bmatrix} e^{\lambda_1 t} \bv_1 \amp \cdots \amp e^{\lambda_n t} \bv_n \end{bmatrix}\tag{3.3.1} \end{equation}

is a fundamental matrix for \(\bx'=\bA\bx\text{.}\)
