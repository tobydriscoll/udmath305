Eigenspaces, unlike eigenvectors, are unique. We have to be a bit careful, though, because we usually express such spaces as the span of basis vectors, and those bases are not themselves unique. For instance, \(\span\left(\twovec{1}{0},\twovec{0}{1}\right)\) and \(\span\left(\twovec{1}{1},\twovec{-1}{1}\right)\) are both equal to \(\Cn{2}\text{.}\)
