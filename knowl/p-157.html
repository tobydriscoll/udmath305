Suppose \(s_1=s_2\) is a double root of (2.3.1). This happens when \(b^2=4ac\text{,}\) and \(s_1=-b/(2a)\text{.}\) In that case our candidate for the general solution is inadequate, because we can add the two terms together. Having \(c_1+c_2\) is no more general than having a single integration constant. Somehow we have to come up with a genuinely different second solution. It turns out that the job is done by \(te^{s_1t}\text{.}\) The proof is just a bunch of algebra: plug it in and show that it works.
