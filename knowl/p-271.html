Now consider \(y''+2y'+10y=40\text{.}\) We get

\begin{equation*} Y(s) = \frac{40}{s(s^2+2s+10)} = \frac{40}{s[(s+1)^2+9]}\text{.} \end{equation*}

We decompose into

\begin{equation*} Y(s) = \frac{A}{s} + \frac{3B+C(s+1)}{(s+1)^2+9}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Once we find the constants, we know that the inverse of this is \(y(t) = A + Be^{-t}\sin(3t) + C e^{-t}\cos(3t)\text{.}\) To find the constants we clear denominators and get

\begin{equation*} 40 = A[(s+1)^2+9] + (3B+Cs+C) s\text{.} \end{equation*}

With \(s=0\) we conclude that \(A=4\text{.}\) From this we find that \(40 = Cs^2+(3B+C)s + 4s^2 +8s + 40\text{,}\) which tells us \(C=-4\) and \(B=-4/3\text{.}\) Hence

\begin{equation*} y(t) = 4 - \frac{4}{3} e^{-t} \sin(3t) - 4 e^{-t} \cos(3t)\text{.} \end{equation*}
