Theorem 3.2.3. Homogeneous solution independence.

Suppose that \(\bA(t)\) is continuous on an open interval \(I\text{,}\) and let \(\bx_1,\ldots,\bx_n\) be solutions of \(\bx'=\bA\bx\) whose Wronskian is \(W(t)\text{.}\) Then if \(W\) is nonzero at any time in \(I\text{,}\) it is nonzero at all times in \(I\text{.}\)
