Solution 2.8.4.2.1.

Suppose we let \(u(t)=x'(t)\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \lx[x''(t)] = \lx[u'(t)] = sU(s) - u(0). \end{equation*}

Also

\begin{equation*} U(s) = \lx[u(t)] = \lx[x'(t)] = sX(s) - x(0). \end{equation*}

Thus

\begin{equation*} \lx[x''(t)] = s[sX(s)-x(0)] - u(0) = s^2X(s) - s x(0) - x'(0). \end{equation*}
