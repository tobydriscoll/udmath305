\begin{equation*} \dd{x}{t} = x^2 \end{equation*}

This is our first example featuring a nonlinear term in the variable \(x\text{.}\) As a rule, nonlinear ODEs are much harder to solve and have less mathematical structure than linear problems. This particular example isn't so tough, though; you can check that \(x=1/(1-t)\) is a solution. Note that the growth is even faster--literally off the charts as \(t\to 1\text{.}\)
