\begin{align*} \cos(\theta) + i \sin(\theta) \amp = e^{i\theta} \\ (\cos(\theta) + i \sin(\theta))^2 \amp = e^{2i\theta} \\ [\cos^2(\theta) - \sin^2(\theta) ] +i[ 2\cos(\theta)\sin(\theta) ] \amp = \cos(2\theta) + i\sin(2\theta) \end{align*}

The imaginary part of both sides gives the first required identity. Multiply by \(e^{i\theta}\) again to get the triple-angle identity.
