The formulas of the theorem are useful mainly as a theoretical tool, a starting point for further derivations, and in specific cases where they can be greatly simplified. But (3.7.3) does have a reasonable interpretation. The term \(e^{t\bA}\bx_0\) is the homogeneous (unforced) response to the initial condition. The integrand \(e^{(t-s)\bA} \bff(s)\) represents the ODE evolution of an initial \(\bff(s)\) through time \(t-s\text{;}\) that is, the forcing at time \(s\) contributes an impulse of amplitude \(\bff(s)\text{.}\) Finally, the integral just adds up all of those impulse contributions from prior times.
