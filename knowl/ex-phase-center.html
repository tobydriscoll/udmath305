Example 3.2.3 Phase portrait of a center

Here is a phase portrait for the equation \(y''+2y=0\text{.}\)

A = 1;  B = 0;  C = 2;
s = roots([A B C]);

plot(0,0,'r.','markersize',18)
    
% Plot trajectories
hold on
colr = get(gca,'colororder');
t = linspace(0,15,1000)';
for R = .2:.2:1
    y = R*real(exp(s(1)*t));
    dydt = R*real(s(1)*exp(s(1)*t));
    plot(y,dydt,'linew',1.5,'color',colr(1,:)), hold on
end
axis([-1.5 1.5 -1.5 1.5]), axis square
title('center')
xlabel('y')
ylabel('y''')

The characteristic roots are imaginary and the solutions are, in shifted cosine form, \(y=R\cos(\omega_n t-\phi)\text{.}\) Solution curves in the phase plane lie on the ellipses \(y^2+(y'/\omega_n)^2=R^2\text{,}\) going around and around the origin, which we call a center of the ODE. If \(\omega_n=1\text{,}\) the ellipses are circles.
