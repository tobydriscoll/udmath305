An eigenvalue is a clean, well-defined target. Eigenvectors can be slipperier. For starters, if \(\bA\bv=\lambda\bv\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} \bA(c\bv) = c(\bA\bv)=c(\lambda\bv)=\lambda(c\bv)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Hence every nonzero multiple of an eigenvector is also an eigenvector for the same \(\lambda\). But there can be even more ambiguity than that.
