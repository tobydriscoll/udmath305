The phase portraits we have done are all in the vicinity of the fixed point at the origin, and they tell the story of stability. Sinks are stable points, while sources and saddles are unstable. The case of a center is on the cusp, what we might call "neutrally stable" or weakly stable. (Many authors use "stable" to mean this type of stability, and "asymptotically stable" to mean the stronger type exhibited by a sink.)
