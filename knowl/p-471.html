The nonlinear pendulum equation \(\theta''+\gamma \theta' + \frac{g}{L}\sin(\theta) = 0\) has an equilibrium at \(\theta=0\text{,}\) which is the downward position. But it also has an equilibrium at the upward or inverted position, \(\theta=\pi\text{.}\) Show that the change of variable \(\phi=\pi-\theta\) leads to

\begin{equation*} \phi'' + \gamma \phi' - \frac{g}{L}\sin(\phi) = 0, \end{equation*}

which has an equilibrium at \(\phi=0\text{.}\) Making the small-angle approximation for sin gives the linear equation

\begin{equation*} \phi'' + \gamma \phi' - \frac{g}{L} \phi = 0. \end{equation*}

Show that for any \(\gamma \ge 0\text{,}\) the equilibrium is an unstable saddle.
