One solution of the IVP \(x'=2\sqrt{x}\text{,}\) \(x(0)=0\) is \(x(t)=t^2\text{.}\) But so is the constant function \(x(t)\equiv 0\text{.}\) In fact, so is \(x(t)=H(t-T)(t-T)^2\) for any positive delay \(T\text{.}\) Hence we cannot automatically expect unique solutions to nonlinear initial-value problems.
