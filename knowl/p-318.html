We can generalize the systems under study a bit to

\begin{align*} \dd{u_1}{t} \amp = a_{11} u_1 + a_{12} u_2 \\ \dd{u_2}{t} \amp = a_{21} u_1 + a_{22} u_2 \text{,} \end{align*}

where the \(a_{ij}\) are all constants. This is a first-order, two-dimensional, linear, constant-coefficient, homogeneous system that we abbreviate as

\begin{equation*} \dd{\mathbf{u}}{t} = \mathbf{A} \mathbf{u}\text{,} \end{equation*}

where \(\mathbf{A}\) is the matrix

\begin{equation*} \mathbf{A} = \begin{bmatrix} a_{11} \amp a_{12} \\ a_{21} \amp a_{22} \end{bmatrix}\text{.} \end{equation*}

As the text says, the matrices we get from problems \(Ay''+By'+Cy=0\) are called companion matrices.
