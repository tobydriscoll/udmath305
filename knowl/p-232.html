Variation of parameters is all that we technically need for linear first-order problems, but as we have seen, it requires some grunt work--in particular, two integrals. There is a significantly shorter path for (2.2.1) in the important special case when (1) the growth/decay rate coefficient \(a(t)\) is constant, and (2) the forcing function is a polynomial, exponential, sin, or cos, or a combination of these. In this situation, the correct \(x_p\) can be written down immediately up to some unknowns or undetermined coefficients. Values for these coefficients are by substitution of \(x_p\) into the ODE.
