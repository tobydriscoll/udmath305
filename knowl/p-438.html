Our main item of business in this chapter is the linear, constant-coefficient model problem

\begin{equation} x'' + bx' + cx = f(t)\text{,}\tag{4.1.1} \end{equation}

for which the conversion process with \(u_1=x\text{,}\) \(u_2=x'\) yields

\begin{equation} \twovec{u_1'}{u_2'} = \twovec{u_2}{f(t)-cu_1-bu_2} = \twomat{0}{1}{-c}{-b} \bu + \twovec{0}{f(t)}\text{,}\tag{4.1.2} \end{equation}

which has the form \(\bu'=\bA\bu+\bff(t)\) for the companion matrix

\begin{equation} \bA = \twomat{0}{1}{-c}{-b}\text{.}\tag{4.1.3} \end{equation}

Thus everything we learned from the last chapter is again in play. However, the applications of (4.1.1) are important and numerous enough to consider the solutions in detail, and the structure of the companion matrix is such that the conclusions can often be rewritten more simply than in the general case.
